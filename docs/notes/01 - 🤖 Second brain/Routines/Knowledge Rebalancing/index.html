Knowledge Rebalancing

Knowledge rebalancing is the periodic review and restructuring of your knowledge base to maintain optimal organization and discoverability.

When to Rebalance

  • Monthly or quarterly reviews
  • When a specific area of knowledge has grown significantly
  • When you find yourself struggling to locate information
  • When notes have grown too large or unwieldy

Rebalancing Process

  1. Identify Areas for Rebalancing:

    • Large notes that cover multiple topics
    • Clusters of very small, related notes that could be combined
    • Areas with poor connectivity between notes
    • Topics that have grown significantly since last review

  2. Apply Appropriate Strategies:

    • Use Chunking to break down large notes
    • Consolidate tiny, related notes into more substantial units
    • Update MOCs to reflect current knowledge structure
    • Improve tagging for better discoverability

  3. Strengthen Connections:

    • Add missing links between related notes
    • Update "Related notes" sections
    • Ensure bidirectional linking
    • Consider creating new MOCs for emerging topic areas

Guidelines

  • Focus on making meaningful improvements, not perfect organization
  • Prioritize areas you actively use or will need soon
  • Balance between atomic notes and connected context
  • Maintain a consistent approach to note structure and metadata
  • Document your rebalancing decisions for future reference
