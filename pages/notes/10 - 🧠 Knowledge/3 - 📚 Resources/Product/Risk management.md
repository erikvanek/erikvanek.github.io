- essential for a successful PM
- first identify all uncertain events that may happen in the project
- risks can be both positive (opportunity) and negative (threat)

## Qualitative phase
- assign each risk two abstract values (e.g. 1–5) for **probability** and **impact**
- multiply both to get a final score for each risk

## Quantitative phase
- exchange probability to percentage points (e.g. if an incident occurs once in 10 years and your project runs for 2, probability = 20%)
- exchange impact for money (or other relevant unit) — e.g. a two-week delay = wages + deadline penalty fees
- quantitative exposure = probability × impact

## Planning risk responses
Three strategies for each type:

- **Negative risks (threats)**
	- Avoid
	- Mitigate
	- Transfer

- **Positive risks (opportunities)**
	- Exploit
	- Enhance
	- Share
