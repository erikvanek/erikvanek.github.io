- ideally happens within 24 to 72 hours after the completion of an event

## 4 steps for effective debriefing (HBR)

1. **Schedule a regular time and place** — make the debrief expected; the more you debrief, the more effective and efficient the process becomes
2. **Create a learning environment** — allow participants to be vulnerable and express weakness; results, both good and bad, should be considered team results
3. **Review 4 key questions**
	- What were we trying to accomplish?
	- Where did we hit or miss our objectives?
	- What caused our results? (root cause analysis, 5 whys)
	- What should we start, stop or continue doing?
4. **Codify lessons learned** — capture in a usable format for later reference

- Usually the biggest hurdle to debriefing is merely starting it — pick small projects and an intimate team to pilot the process
