Goals are usually stated as questions and help to assess various aspects of interactive products.

1. **Effectiveness** — how well the product does what it is intended to do. *"Is the product capable of allowing people to carry out their work efficiently?"*
2. **Efficiency** — the way the product supports people carrying out their tasks. *"How many steps are needed to perform a given task?"*
3. **Safety** — protecting a person from dangerous conditions; preventing dangerous errors; boosting users' confidence. *"What is the range of errors users can face? What are possible ways to recover?"*
4. **Utility** — the extent to which the product provides the right kind of functionality
5. **Learnability** — how easy it is to learn to use the system. *"Is it possible to learn to accomplish main tasks by just exploring the product on my own?"*
6. **Memorability** — the ability to remember how the system works once learned. *"What types of support does the product offer to help users remember how to use it?"*
7. **Satisfaction** — how acceptable the product is once used. *"What is the NPS? How does satisfaction change over time?"*
