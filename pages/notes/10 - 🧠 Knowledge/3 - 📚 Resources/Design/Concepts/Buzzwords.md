## DIX
- disability interactions (see [[Ability]])

## Microinteraction
- trigger-feedback pairs in which (1) the trigger can be a user action or an alteration in the system's state; (2) the feedback is a narrowly targeted response to the trigger, communicated through small, highly contextual (usually visual) changes in the UI

## Affordances
- visual or logical cues that tools and products show us about how to use them
- action possibilities in the environment
- when an object gives cues that go against how it really works, that is known as providing an _incorrect affordance_

## Progressive disclosure
- providing only the information people need at the moment and letting them click to learn more as they want
- worth sacrificing the "minimisation of clicks" metric for clarity

## Cognitive dissonance denial
- not allowing any incoming information that conflicts with an internal belief
- two main ways to beat cognitive dissonance:
	- change your belief
	- deny one of the options
