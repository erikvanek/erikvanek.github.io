- humans face loads — ordered by how demanding they are:
	- **cognitive** (most demanding)
	- **visual**
	- **motor** — Fitts's law defines how big objects on screen should be; a relationship between speed, accuracy, and distance between objects; good to minimize motor switching (mouse to keyboard and vice versa)
- mostly we want to reduce loads, but when there's a need for more focus, we should increase them

## Mental models
- a collection of thought processes and memories about how something works
- they drive behavior

## Attention
- **Assume you have at most 7 to 10 minutes of a person's attention.**
- If you must hold attention longer than 7–10 minutes, introduce novel information or a break
