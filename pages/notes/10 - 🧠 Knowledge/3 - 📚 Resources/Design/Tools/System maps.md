- representation of the main constituents of a system
- people, stakeholders, services, products, platforms, KPIs, ...
- complex systems become more comprehensible when visualised
- based on systems theory & thinking

## Types

### Stakeholder maps
- answer "Which important people & stakeholders are involved in the experience?"
- understanding who is involved & how they are interconnected
- help to map stakeholders and relationships between them or possible frictions
- during customer journeys, users interact with various stakeholders

### Value network maps
- extension of stakeholder maps
- details out the exchanges between stakeholders
- illustrates exchanges between stakeholders — value can be money, data, information

### Ecosystem maps
- extension of value network maps
- involve more constituents e.g. machines, platforms, systems
- include also non-human interactions
- often need different zoom levels due to complexity
