{{title}}

I'm developing a new childcare service called Sousedské dětské skupiny in collaboration with the Ministry of Social Affairs. Inspiration for this home-based childcare comes from services like Tagesmutter in Germany or Austria.

Our goal is to create an easy-to-use service that provides care in the welcoming homes of caregivers. By offering small-group settings, we prioritize personal attention and foster a family-like atmosphere.

We are committed to understanding and meeting the needs of children, their families and service providers i.e. caregivers. We actively listen to their needs, potential roadblocks and feedback to shape our service better. We actively work with these data to ensure that the leadership can make the most informed decisions possible to make the service more customer-centric.

Alongside this project, we are enhancing the design maturity of the organization. By fostering collaboration, knowledge-sharing, and promoting customer-centricity, we strive to empower the Ministry of Social Affairs to embrace innovative, human-centered design approaches and techniques.

Project goals

Understanding user and stakeholder needs

Through user research, we gain insights into the needs, motivations, and barriers of primary user groups and key stakeholders. This understanding helps us position the service effectively and sets clear boundaries for designing processes and tools.

Stakeholder alignment

Designing a public service involves aligning multiple stakeholders with different motivations, fears, and expectations. We work towards understanding each perspective to move the project forward effectively.

Service prototyping

We embrace an iterative, feedback-driven approach throughout the project. Storyboarding helps us empathize with user needs and identify process improvements. We test various artifacts rapidly to ensure they are delivered in the best possible way.

Human-centered design advocacy

By observing and understanding the challenges faced by project teams in bringing human-centered designs, we gather valuable insights for the organization. We document, share, and find ways to improve conditions for future innovation initiatives.

Key outcomes

Pilot project development and facilitation

We created and conducted a comprehensive pilot project structure to validate the service proposition in the field. The aim of the pilot project is to simulate the service before the legislation is fully estabilished and approved. By facilitating key events and ensuring smooth execution, we successfully led the initiative from conception to completion.

Fostering remote collaboration

Despite the ministry's focus on in-person interactions, we created an efficient remote collaboration environment. Our ability to adapt and deliver successful outcomes, even in challenging circumstances, highlights our flexibility and problem-solving skills.

Promoting public participation

Through our facilitation of several round table and focus group sessions, we effectively included key stakeholders in the project. This inclusive approach garnered valuable insights and demonstrated our commitment to stakeholder engagement and public participation.

Building key partnerships

We prioritized establishing close contacts with key stakeholders, such as the ministry deputy, recognizing that top-level support is crucial for project success. By fostering collaboration with these influential actors, we were able to define and pursue critical project priorities.

Implementing iterative processes

Our team introduced an iterative approach to key activities, enabling faster feedback loops than typically experienced in the ministry environment. By inviting broader public collaboration, we showcased our ability to co-design public policies in a participative manner, leaving a positive impact and setting a new standard for engagement.
