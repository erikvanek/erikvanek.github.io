  • an approach to scientific inquiry using insights to better understand wicked problems
    • many problems can never be accurately modeled, thus an engineering approach to addressing them would fail
  • Basically doing design as a research project
  • field researchers go after these things in places where the design is supposed to be used
  • using methods from ethnography, cultural probes (cameras and diaries), and methods from social science
  • In RtD, researchers build prototypes and are then able to learn what it would be like if the product existed, to act as though it exists, ideally in the place it would be used
  • The goal is to provide stories that tell how people experience the design and what trains of thought came up

Alain Findeli's three branches

  • research for design — helps to guide and develop design practice ~ traditional research during human-centered design
  • research into design — research studying design itself
  • research through design — recasting the design aspect of creation as research; practitioners actually create new products, experiment with materials, processes etc

Key principles

  • research in design is not concerned with the "true" but with the "real"
  • researcher learns about the object of their inquiry through the constant evolution of the artefact — it "allows for creating a dialog with the material"
  • Theoretical sensitivity — the investigator's ability to use personal and professional experiences and the literature to see the research situation and data in new ways
  • Recoverability (validity criterion) — the designer/researcher must make sure that "the process is recoverable by anyone interested in subjecting the research to critical scrutiny"
  • The reality described by RtD is the one that 'will be' — the preferred state

Flow of Research through Design

  • Coupling — unites research and design interests; establishes basic frame and constraints
  • Interweaving — research interests and design interests influence each other; processes, methods and validation are established
  • Decoupling — at later points, focus shifts to one interest set (e.g., during production = design focus; during final evaluation = research focus)

Notes

  • a good RtD project first explains how the world is today, and defines a clear direction — a new state of the world. You need to have a vision.
  • RtD is more of a way of explaining or labeling what you've done, rather than a recipe for doing it
